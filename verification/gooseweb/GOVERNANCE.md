# Migration review governance

Every P01-P56 browser review uses one migration-wide exclusive stack lease. Lease IDs are globally unique and sortable, sequences are strictly increasing, and acquisition/release intervals may not overlap across phase or P56 integration clearances. Before acquisition, the supervisor records termination evidence for the prior lease's managed process. Release is valid only after the current managed process has stopped and cleanup has completed.

The supervisor alone owns `DEV_DIR`, runtime/Goosetower/Gooseweb ports, source and configuration, exact clean branch/head/tree, served head, mode, managed process identity, start, stop, and cleanup while holding the slot. A changed tree, manifest path/hash/revision, lease, stack configuration, served head, or reviewer invalidates clearance. Dirty-tree and hot-reload evidence are forbidden. P56 enters the same queue only after every phase lease is released; it cannot overlap a phase lease.

The assigned implementer hands off a clean committed candidate. One separate reviewer then attaches read-only and owns only a globally unique named real-Chromium `agent-browser` session and evidence. The reviewer must not edit, format, stage, commit, switch branches, reset, clean, build, generate, publish, or control a server. Implementer and reviewer alternate; mutation, build, format, and generation never overlap review.

For a fix, the supervisor stops, cleans, and releases before the implementer mutates. The new clean head queues and reacquires the global lease. Routine findings go directly from reviewer to implementer; they do not route through the supervisor or lead. The reviewer sends final exact-head clearance only to the lead and never sends approval to the implementer. The lead validates the tuple and alone authorizes shipping; the supervisor does not integrate main. A replacement reviewer reruns the entire review.

P01-P05 may clear verification infrastructure with `known_defects: []` while finite pre-existing product failures remain in `baseline_detected`. Each baseline names a stable scenario, first divergent layer, evidence, one P06-P10 correction phase, downstream gates, and `blocked_not_approved`. Baselines never waive infrastructure, security, leakage, or regression failures and never constitute product approval.
